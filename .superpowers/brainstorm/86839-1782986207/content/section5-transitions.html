Section 5 — Page Transitions & Scroll Effects

How the page feels in motion — entrances, exits, and scroll-triggered animations.

Page entrance

CURRENT: simple fadeIn (opacity + translateY 8px)
PROPOSED OPTIONS:
A: Enhanced fadeIn — longer duration, deeper translateY, staggered children B: Glitch entrance — content flickers/garbles briefly then resolves into place C: Scan reveal — horizontal wipe reveals content from bottom to top D: Terminal boot — content "loads" line by line with a boot sequence feel
A

Enhanced fadeIn with stagger

Smooth, elegant. Cards/sections appear one after another. Best balance.

B

Glitch entrance

Content appears garbled for 200ms then snaps clean. High impact, could annoy.

C

Scanline reveal

Horizontal bar sweeps up revealing content. Feels like a scanner booting up.

D

Terminal boot sequence

Content types out row by row. Maximum cyberpunk, slowest to load.

Scroll effects

A

Parallax layers

Background circuit patterns move slower than content on scroll. Depth illusion.

B

Scroll-triggered reveal

Elements fade/slide in as they enter viewport. Already partially done.

C

Reading progress bar

A thin glowing bar at page top tracks scroll depth. Cyberpunk signal meter.

D

Image reveal on scroll

Cover images "develop" from noise/static as you scroll down. Glitch-to-clear.

E

Data-stream scroll background

Subtle matrix-like characters flowing in background, speed varies with scroll.

Navigation transitions

A

Instant (current)

Standard Next.js client-side routing. Fast, reliable.

B

Fade transition

Brief 150ms crossfade between pages. Smooth upgrade.

C

Glitch exit

Page briefly distorts/flickers on exit before next page loads. Punk feel.
